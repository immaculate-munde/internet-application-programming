Feminine Power & Leadership

Celebrating the pioneers, the fighters, and the girls' girls.

The Fight for Equality

Every single day, women across the globe are fighting to be considered for the same opportunities as their male counterparts. From the boardroom to the laboratory, the journey has been long, but our resolve is stronger than ever.

We must actively unlearn the idea that women belong on the sidelines. Instead, we know that women belong in all places where decisions are being made.

It is vital that we embrace being a "girl's girl." This means lifting each other up, celebrating each other's wins, and recognizing that another woman's success is not your failure. When we stand together, we are an unstoppable force of nature.

Pioneers Who Paved the Way

We stand on the shoulders of giants. Let's look at a few historical icons who shattered glass ceilings in STEM and activism.

  1. Ada Lovelace

    Often referred to as the Mother of Programming, Ada realized that computers could do much more than just crunch numbers.
    Read more about Ada's algorithmic genius

  2. Wangari Maathai

    The first African woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize, Wangari founded the Green Belt Movement. She famously said, It’s the little things citizens do. That’s what will make the difference.
    Explore Wangari's environmental legacy

  3. Valentina Tereshkova

    A true pioneer of the stars, she was the very first female astronaut to travel into space, orbiting the Earth 48 times in 1963.
    Discover more women in space via NASA

  4. Grace Hopper

    A trailblazer in computer science, Rear Admiral Grace Hopper was one of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer and helped invent the first programming language compiler. She famously helped popularize the term "computer bug."
    Learn about Grace's legendary tech career

  5. Katherine Johnson

    A brilliant mathematician whose precise calculations of orbital mechanics as a NASA employee were absolutely critical to the success of the first U.S. crewed spaceflights. Her story is a testament to unyielding intellect in the face of discrimination.
    Read Katherine's NASA biography

  6. Malala Yousafzai

    A fierce, modern-day advocate for girls' education and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate in history. She reminds us all of the power of our voices, stating, One child, one teacher, one book, one pen can change the world.
    Discover Malala's continuing fight

  7. Marie Curie

    A pioneering physicist and chemist who conducted breakthrough research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, and she remains the only person to ever win a Nobel Prize in two different scientific fields!
    Explore Marie Curie's groundbreaking work
